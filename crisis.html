Are you facing an emotional crisis?


Understanding Emotional Crisis, Severe Depression, and Mental Breakdowns

Life can be challenging, and sometimes the weight of it all can lead to emotional crises, severe depression, or even mental breakdowns. It's crucial to recognize the signs and take action to seek professional help. If you or someone you know is experiencing overwhelming emotions, here are some resources that can provide support:


NHS 111

Remember to call 999 if you feel you are in immediate danger!

If you're in the UK and need urgent mental health advice but it's not an emergency, you can call NHS 111 or visit their website.

Shout Text Service

Text SHOUT to 85258 for confidential support. The Shout Text Service offers a safe space to talk about whatever you're going through, 24/7.

C.A.L.M. (Campaign Against Living Miserably)

For men who may be struggling, C.A.L.M. provides a helpline at 0800 585858. They offer support, information, and a helpline specifically dedicated to preventing male suicide.

Samaritans

The Samaritans provide a free helpline at 116 123, available 24/7. You can talk to them about anything that's troubling you; they offer a non-judgmental and confidential space to share your feelings.

Find A Helpline

If you need more options or specialized help, you can explore Find A Helpline's database, which provides a comprehensive list of helplines addressing various mental health concerns.

Remember, seeking help is a sign of strength, and there is support available. If you or someone you know is struggling, don't hesitate to reach out to these resources. You are not alone.
